Equally Sharing a Cake Between Three People - Numberphile

One-Line Description

A mathematical exploration of how to divide a cake fairly among three people using an "envy-free" algorithm, complete with actual cake cutting and a delightful tangent about audiobook recording.

Summary

This Numberphile video tackles the surprisingly complex problem of fair cake division among three people. While splitting between two people is straightforward (one cuts, one chooses), three people require a more sophisticated approach. The video demonstrates a recently discovered "envy-free" algorithm where Alice first cuts the cake into three pieces she considers equal, then Bob trims his favorite piece to match his second favorite, Charlie picks first, followed by Bob, then Alice. The leftover trimming goes through the same process in reverse order. The presenter manages to make abstract mathematics tangible through actual cake cutting, though the demonstration reveals that even mathematicians struggle with straight knife work. The video concludes with the sobering revelation that this algorithm scales horrifically - for n people, you need n^n^n^n^n^n cuts, which is "kind of bonkers."

Conclusions

The video successfully demonstrates that fair, envy-free cake division is mathematically possible for three people, though it requires a specific multi-step algorithm. However, the practical application becomes increasingly absurd as the number of people increases, with the cut count exploding to incomprehensible levels. The method ensures mathematical fairness but at the cost of practicality - a perfect example of how elegant mathematical solutions can be utterly impractical in real life.
